Supplement stack
Essential
Whey protein (isolate)
40–50g post-workout + as needed
Fastest way to hit protein targets without excess calories. Isolate preferred for absorption speed and digestive ease.
Essential
Creatine monohydrate
5g daily, any time, with food
The most proven supplement for natural lifters. Increases strength, work capacity and muscle cell volume. Take every day regardless of whether you train.
Recommended
Vitamin D3 + K2
3,000–5,000 IU D3 + 100mcg K2 daily
Despite living in Australia, early starts and office time mean deficiency is common. Critical for testosterone production, bone health and immune function.
Recommended
Magnesium glycinate
400mg before bed
Improves sleep quality, reduces muscle cramps and supports testosterone. Deficiency is common in hard-training athletes. Glycinate form is easiest on the stomach.
Recommended
Omega-3 fish oil
2–3g EPA+DHA daily with meals
Reduces inflammation from heavy training, supports joint health and improves muscle protein synthesis signalling. Take with your main dinner meal.
Recommended
Caffeine / pre-workout
150–200mg, 30 min pre-session
Given your early starts, evening training can feel sluggish. Caffeine reliably improves strength output and focus. Plain caffeine tabs are cleaner than most pre-workouts.
Optional
Zinc (ZMA)
30mg elemental zinc before bed
Supports testosterone and IGF-1 in hard-training athletes. Take away from food. Can be combined with magnesium as a ZMA formula.
Optional
Digestive enzymes
1 capsule with large meals
Eating high volume as a hardgainer can cause bloating. Enzymes aid digestion and absorption — worth trying if large meals leave you uncomfortable.
Priority order: creatine and protein first, then D3/K2 and magnesium, then the rest once nutrition is locked in. Don't buy commercial mass gainers — they are mostly sugar. Build your own from oats, whole milk, banana, whey and peanut butter for a fraction of the cost.
